About Our Barangay

Our Community Story

For over 62 years, Barangay 118 has stood as a pillar of public service — committed to delivering efficient, transparent, and accessible government services to our residents. Located in the heart of the city, our barangay is home to a vibrant community of families, businesses, and institutions working together for progress and unity.

Through the launch of our Online System, we are taking our commitment to the next level — providing 24/7 access to essential services from the comfort of your home. This digital transformation bridges the gap between the barangay administration and the people we serve, ensuring convenience and connectivity for all.

Behind every program and project is a dedicated team of officials and staff who serve with integrity, transparency, and compassion — ensuring that every resident feels heard, supported, and cared for.

Document Requirements

Information about the documents you can request and their requirements

Barangay Clearance

Required for various transactions including employment, business permits, and legal matters. This document certifies your good standing in the community.

Requirements:

  • Valid government-issued ID
  • Proof of Residency (utility bill or lease agreement)
  • Community Tax Certificate (cedula)

Processing Time: 10-15 minutes

Certificate of Residency

Proof of residency in Barangay 118 for various purposes such as school enrollment, loan applications, and government transactions.

Requirements:

  • Valid government-issued ID
  • Proof of Residency (utility bill or barangay ID)
  • Completed application form

Processing Time: Same day

Certificate of Indigency

For availing government assistance programs and benefits. This document certifies your eligibility for social welfare programs.

Requirements:

  • Valid ID
  • Proof of Income or Affidavit of No Income
  • Proof of Residency

Processing Time: 1-2 days

Barangay ID

Official identification card issued by Barangay 118. Valid for local transactions and as supplementary identification.

Requirements:

  • Valid ID (if available)
  • Proof of Residency
  • 1x1 ID Photo
  • Completed application form

Processing Time: 3-5 working days

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our barangay and services

What are the office hours of the barangay hall?

Our barangay hall is open from Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, and on Saturdays from 8:00 AM to 12:00 PM. We are closed on Sundays and holidays.

How can I request a Barangay Clearance?

Required documents: Valid government-issued ID (driver's license, passport, voter's ID), proof of residency (utility bill, lease/rental contract), completed barangay clearance application form, Community Tax Certificate (cedula), and passport-size photo if required.
Fees: Free (no payment required).
Processing time: Usually issued immediately (10–15 minutes).
Where & How to apply: Apply in person at Barangay 118 Hall, weekdays 8 AM–5 PM. Submit requirements and receive your clearance.

How can I request a Certificate of Residency?

Required documents: Government-issued ID, proof of address (utility bill, lease, or cedula), and completed residency certificate form.
Fees: Free (no payment required).
Processing time: On the spot or same day.
Where & How to apply: Apply at Barangay 118 Hall, weekdays 8 AM–5 PM. Present your documents and receive the certificate.

Eligibility / Rights:
- Possessory Rights: Individuals occupying a property under recognized occupancy (e.g., informal settlers with proof of residence).
- Ownership Rights: Individuals who legally own the property where they reside.

How can I request a Certificate of Indigency?

Required documents: Government-issued ID, proof of residence (barangay ID, voter's record, or utility bill), supporting income documents if applicable, and purpose statement (e.g., "for medical assistance").
Fees: Free (no payment required).
Processing time: Usually issued immediately.
Where & How to apply: Submit request at Barangay 118 Hall, weekdays 8 AM–5 PM. The officer will review and issue the certificate.

How can I get a Barangay ID?

Required documents: Valid ID (if any), proof of residency (utility bill, barangay certificate, or cedula), 1–2 passport-size photos, and completed application form.
Fees: Free (no payment required).
Processing time: Usually issued on the same day.
Where & How to apply: Apply at Barangay 118 Hall, weekdays 8 AM–5 PM. Submit your requirements and receive your Barangay ID.

How can I report a community concern?

You can report concerns by visiting the Barangay 118 Hall, calling our office at (02) 8123-4567, or using the contact form on our website. For emergencies, please contact the proper emergency services first.

You may also visit the "Get In Touch" section on our website and send your message there. Our team will review it and assist you accordingly.
